Tourist Attractions in Semarang

Sam Poo Kong

A historic Chinese temple built to honor the legendary Chinese Muslim admiral, Cheng Ho, who visited Semarang during his voyages.

Pasar Semawis

A lively night market offering a wide variety of street food, especially Chinese-Indonesian delicacies, set in Semarang's Chinatown. Itâ€™s usually only open during weekend nights.

Goa Kreo

A cave and cultural park associated with Sunan Kalijaga, one of the Wali Songo in Javanese folklore, located near Jatibarang Reservoir. It offers panoramic views and houses monkeys considered sacred by locals.

Lawang Sewu

A historic Chinese temple built to honor the legendary Chinese Muslim admiral, Cheng Ho, who visited Semarang during his voyages.

Kota Lama Semarang

The "Old Town" of Semarang, featuring colonial Dutch architecture, quaint cafes, and art galleries, evoking a bygone era. It is also where Gereja Blenduk, which is the oldest church in Central Java, is located.

Tugu Muda

A monument in Semarang that commemorates the heroic efforts of Indonesian fighters during the Battle of Five Days against the Japanese army in 1945.
